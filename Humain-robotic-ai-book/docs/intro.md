# Introduction

Welcome to **Physical AI & Robotics** - your comprehensive guide to building intelligent humanoid robots.

## About This Book

This educational resource bridges the gap between digital intelligence and physical embodiment, covering the complete stack from low-level robot control to high-level AI reasoning.

Physical AI represents the frontier where artificial intelligence meets the real world. Here's why this field is transforming industries and society:

## What You'll Learn

- **ROS 2 Foundation**: Master the robotic operating system for distributed control
- **Digital Twin Simulation**: Create realistic physics and visual simulations
- **Perception & Navigation**: Implement visual SLAM and autonomous navigation
- **Vision-Language-Action**: Integrate AI models for natural human-robot interaction

### Who This Is For

This book is designed for:
- Robotics students and researchers
- Software engineers transitioning to robotics
- AI practitioners interested in embodied intelligence
- Anyone building humanoid robots with ROS 2

## Prerequisites

Before starting, you should have:

- **Programming**: Python 3.8+ and basic C++ knowledge
- **ROS 2**: Humble Hawksbill or newer installed ([Installation Guide](https://docs.ros.org/en/humble/Installation.html))
- **Linux**: Ubuntu 22.04 LTS (or Docker/WSL2 on Windows/Mac)
- **Hardware**: Modern GPU recommended for simulation (NVIDIA preferred)

## Course Structure

The book is organized into 4 progressive modules:

1. **ROS 2 Robotic Nervous System** - Foundation layer for robot communication
2. **Digital Twin** - Simulation environments (Gazebo & Unity)
3. **Perception & Navigation** - Spatial awareness and autonomous movement
4. **Vision-Language-Action** - AI-powered cognitive capabilities

Each module contains practical examples, code snippets, and hands-on exercises.

## Getting Started

Start with **Module 1: ROS 2 Robotic Nervous System** to build your foundation, then progress sequentially through the modules.
